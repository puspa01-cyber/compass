==================================================
1. ONE-LINE SUMMARY
==================================================
Gannon Compass is a student-first campus guide for Gannon University that packages practical college life information into an editorial, confident, friendly, and highly skimmable digital experience.

==================================================

3. Events
Purpose: surface relevant campus happenings
Content pattern: category, date, time, location, why it matters

4. Academic Tips
Purpose: turn generic study advice into useful campus-specific guidance
Content pattern: short principle + one practical explanation + link to more

5. Contact / Contributions
Purpose: make the site feel alive and community-fed
Content pattern: prompts for missing spots, events, or tips

11. COLOR SYSTEM (REVERSE-ENGINEERED)
==================================================
A. Core brand direction
The strongest brand anchor is Gannon’s maroon-and-gold identity, translated into a more modern, editorial web experience.

B. Recreation palette
These are practical recreation values, not guaranteed source-code values.

Primary Maroon
Hex: #862633
Use for: brand anchors, emphasis, section accents, buttons, small highlights

Accent Gold
Hex: #F1B434
Use for: secondary accents, warmth, badges, subtle emphasis

Off-White / Warm Canvas
Hex: #F7F4EE
Use for: page backgrounds, large breathing areas

Charcoal / Ink
Hex: #111111
Use for: headings, primary text, strong contrast

Slate
Hex: #4C5762
Use for: secondary text, dividers, subdued interface elements

Stone
Hex: #CCC9B8
Use for: card surfaces, borders, muted backgrounds

Warm Gray
Hex: #918F8A
Use for: metadata, low-emphasis labels

Campus Rust
Hex: #712C27
Use for: occasional warm accent pulled from campus seating/brick imagery

Olive Support
Hex: #7D8A4A
Use for: environmental accent pulled from campus interiors and landscape tones

Blue-Gray Light
Hex: #C3C9C1
Use for: quiet panels and spacious neutral fills

C. Practical usage guidance
Neutrals should dominate the UI.
Maroon should function as the strongest branded accent.
Gold should be used sparingly so it feels premium, not noisy.
Photography can introduce environmental warmth: brick, rust, olive, slate, daylight.

Suggested ratio for recreation:
65% neutrals
20% dark text / charcoal
10% maroon
5% gold and environmental accents

20. RECREATION GUIDELINES FOR DESIGN OR BUILD
==================================================
If rebuilding or extending the brand, keep these truths intact:

1. Usefulness beats decoration.
2. Voice should sound like a smart student editor.
3. Every section should answer a practical question.
4. Photography should feel real and local.
5. Maroon-and-gold identity should be present but restrained.
6. Layout should prioritize scan speed.
7. Cards and labels should do most of the UX work.
8. The experience should feel current, participatory, and credible.

==================================================
21. QUICK CREATIVE DIRECTION IN ONE PARAGRAPH
==================================================
Create a student-first campus guide brand that feels like the intersection of a university survival guide, a student newspaper feature package, and a clean editorial web product. Use a restrained neutral base, maroon as the primary brand accent, gold sparingly, and real campus imagery for proof and warmth. Write in short, practical, slightly witty copy. Organize information around immediate student needs: food, focus, events, and academic success. The final experience should feel useful in under ten seconds.
